This information should include but not limited to: Checking the amount of the annual property taxes and also finding out if there are any arrears in terms of taxes Finding out if there are any restrictions on the title to the property Equally important is checking with your bank and making sure that you are eligible for the mortgage financing amount that you will need

A mortgage is a charge on land created for securing a debt or loan. In other words, a bank or mortgage company lends you the money to buy your home and in turn you sign documents which are registered on the title to your home promising to repay them. If you default on your mortgage, the home will be repossessed to repay the loan, through a legal procedure called foreclosure.

Mortgage companies, banks, trust companies lend money in the form of mortgages. It is a good idea to start with your bank, as they will already be familiar with your credit rating and financial situation. However, do shop around for the best deal as it will offer options in terms of interest rates, payment options flexibility, and so on. At times, if you obtain a better deal elsewhere, your bank may be willing to match it. You can also get a mortgage privately, from your parents for example, or anyone who is willing to lend money on the basis of a mortgage arrangement.
